    Nainital: The Lake District of India


    Nestled around the pristine Naini Lake, Nainital is a charming hill station in Uttarakhand, known as the "Lake District of India." Surrounded by lush green hills and offering panoramic views of the Himalayas, Nainital attracts travelers with its serene beauty and pleasant weather.


    The town is famous for its colonial heritage, scenic lakes, and vibrant bazaars. Landmarks like the Naini Devi Temple, Snow View Point, and Tiffin Top reflect the town's cultural and natural richness.


    Visitors can enjoy boating on Naini Lake, exploring the Mall Road, and soaking in the tranquil atmosphere. Whether you're seeking a romantic getaway, a family vacation, or a solo adventure, Nainital has something for everyone.


    v i s i t - t i m e & i t i n e r a r i e s



    Best Time to Visit:


    • Summers (March–June): Temperatures range between 11°C and 28°C, making it ideal for sightseeing, boating, and trekking. The weather is pleasant, and the blooming flowers add to the charm.

    • Monsoons (July–September): The town experiences moderate to heavy rainfall, turning the surroundings lush green. Perfect for nature lovers, though landslides can affect travel.

    • Winters (October–February): The temperature can drop to 0°C, with snowfall adding a magical touch. Ideal for honeymooners and those who enjoy winter sports.

    Itinerary


    3-Day Itinerary


    • Day 1: Start with boating on Naini Lake, followed by a visit to Naina Devi Temple. Head to Mall Road for shopping and enjoy local delicacies.
    • Day 2: Visit Snow View Point for breathtaking Himalayan views. Explore the Eco Cave Gardens and relax at Tiffin Top for a sunset picnic.
    • Day 3: Take a short trip to Bhimtal and Sattal for more lake adventures. End your trip with a visit to Kilbury Bird Sanctuary.

    4-Day Itinerary

    Includes 3-day itinerary

    • Day 4: Visit Governor’s House (Raj Bhavan) and St. John’s Church. Conclude your trip with local shopping at Bara Bazaar.

    5-Day Itinerary

    Includes 4-day itinerary

    • Day 5: Spend a day in Bhimtal, Sattal, and Naukuchiatal, enjoying kayaking and birdwatching. Discover Pangot’s serene landscapes. Take a trek to Land’s End or Dorothy’s Seat for captivating views. Wrap up with a relaxing evening at Bara Bazaar for souvenirs.



    F o o d & c u s i n e

    Local Cuisine



    Savor the flavors of Bal Mithai, a traditional sweet coated in sugar balls, Bhatt ki Churdkani, a delicious black bean curry, and Ras.

    Street Food


    Relish momos, spicy aloo ke gutke (stir-fried potatoes), and roasted bhutta (corn on the cob).

    Cafes and Bakeries


    Visit popular bakeries like Sakley’s and Pattie’s for their delectable cakes, pastries, and coffee

    Dining options


    Restaurants like Sher-e-Punjab and Machan offer a mix of North Indian, Kumaoni, and international cuisines.

    Naini Lake

    Description: The centerpiece of Nainital, this crescent-shaped lake offers a picturesque setting for boating, kayaking, and paddle boating.

    Surroundings: Surrounded by seven hills, the lake is particularly mesmerizing during sunrise and sunset.

    Activities: Evening strolls along its banks are a must for soaking in the serene beauty.

    Naina Devi Temple

    Location: Located on the northern shore of Naini Lake.

    Dedication: This revered Hindu temple is dedicated to Goddess Naina Devi.

    Surroundings: The peaceful surroundings and stunning views of the lake make it a popular spiritual and tourist spot.

    Festivals: Festivals like Navratri add a vibrant charm to the temple.

    Snow View Point

    Access: Accessible by cable car or a short trek.

    Views: This viewpoint offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks.

    Telescopes: Telescopes at the site provide a closer look at the distant mountains, making it a paradise for photographers.

    Eco Cave Gardens

    Attraction: This cluster of interconnected caves and hanging gardens is a unique attraction for families and adventure enthusiasts.

    Themes: Each cave is themed around an animal, providing an educational and fun experience for children.

    Tiffin Top (Dorothy’s Seat)

    Popularity: A popular picnic spot.

    Views: Tiffin Top offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding hills.

    Trek: The 4-km trek is moderate and rewarding, with ample opportunities for photography and quiet reflection amidst nature.

    Adventure Fairs

    Timing: During summer.

    Activities: The town organizes boat races, paragliding events, and trekking challenges.

    Opportunities: These activities provide opportunities to experience the adventurous side of Nainital.




    h o w - t o - r e a c h


    By Air


    Pantnagar Airport is the nearest airport, located about 65 km from Nainital. Regular flights connect it to major cities.


    By Train


    Kathgodam Railway Station is the nearest railhead, about 34 km from Nainital. It is well-connected to major cities, making train travel a convenient option.


    By Road


    Nainital is accessible via a network of roads. Buses and taxis operate from nearby cities like Delhi, making road travel easy and affordable.




    a c t i v i t i e s



    Boating on Naini Lake

    Boating on Naini Lake

    Choose between paddle boats, rowboats, or yachting to enjoy the tranquil waters of the lake. The experience is especially enchanting during sunrise or sunset, with the hills casting their reflection on the water.


    Trekking

    Trekking

    Trails like Snow View Point, Tiffin Top, and Land’s End offer varying levels of difficulty and stunning vistas. These treks are a great way to connect with nature and explore the lesser-known corners of Nainital.


    Birdwatching at Kilbury and Pangot

    Birdwatching at Kilbury and Pangot

    Known for their rich biodiversity, these spots are home to over 580 species of birds. Pack your binoculars and a guidebook to make the most of this experience.


    Adventure Activities

    Adventure Activities

    Engage in paragliding, zip-lining, and kayaking around Nainital for a thrilling outdoor experience. These activities are perfect for adrenaline junkies.



    l o c a l - m a r k e t s

    Mall Road

    A bustling hub for tourists, Mall Road is lined with shops selling woolen clothes, handcrafted candles, and souvenirs. Street food stalls and cafes make it a lively spot to explore.

    Bara Bazaar

    Famous for its fresh produce, jams, and pickles, Bara Bazaar is a must-visit for food enthusiasts. The local organic honey and spices are highly recommended.

    Tibetan Market

    This vibrant market offers a range of items, from clothing and accessories to traditional Tibetan artifacts. Bargaining is common here, so hone your skills!

    Specialty Items

    Look for intricately carved wooden items, hand-knit woolens, and aromatic herbal teas that reflect the region’s craftsmanship.
